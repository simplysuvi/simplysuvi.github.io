More than a title.

I'm Suvrat — a data scientist, builder, and naturally curious human. I enjoy working on ideas that blend logic with creativity, and I'm someone who learns best by doing. I've always appreciated clean design, honest work, and people who ask good questions. I'm originally from India, and now working in the U.S., trying to create things that feel meaningful. My approach to life is pretty simple: stay curious, work hard, and be kind. Whether I'm debugging a model or brainstorming a new project, I like to take my time to understand the "why" behind things. I believe in growth, but not at the cost of joy.

My approach combines technical expertise with creative problem-solving. I believe the most powerful insights often emerge at the intersection of rigorous analysis and innovative thinking. Whether I'm building predictive models, creating data visualizations, or developing end-to-end machine learning pipelines, I'm driven by the potential to create solutions that make a meaningful impact.

How It All Compiled.

Tech came into my life like a quiet wave — slow at first, then all-consuming. I began learning to code in high school, curious about what made things tick. That early spark turned into a full-blown career as I studied engineering, explored data science, and started building real-world tools. I found a rhythm in systems, algorithms, and visualizations — in making the abstract feel intuitive. Now I work on everything from AI assistants to computer vision tools — and I still get excited about learning something new every day.

W.O.W — World Outside Work.

I like keeping life simple — but full.
Music is where I return to myself. I write, sing, and play guitar whenever I can. Some songs have found a home on Spotify. Others live quietly in my voice notes — raw, unfiltered, just for me. It's less about perfection, more about presence.
I've always been drawn to stories. The ones that stay with you long after the credits roll or the final page turns. Books that shift your perspective. Films that stir something deeper.
And then, there's travel. I don't chase landmarks. I follow feeling. The stillness of a quiet street. A café where no one knows your name. The space between where you are and who you're becoming. Stepping away from routine helps me reconnect — with myself, with new ideas, with the world as it is.
These moments outside of work don't just ground me. They shape how I show up — more present, more curious, more human.
